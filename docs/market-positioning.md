# Kaze Market Positioning

## Core Market Idea

In many real venue markets, customers do not reach hotels, conference rooms, or wedding venues directly.
They often go through informal middlemen, brokers, or commission-based connectors who:

- add friction
- slow down communication
- reduce pricing transparency
- sometimes increase the final price for the customer

Kaze can reduce that dependency by connecting customers, organizers, and venues more directly.

## Strategic Claim

Kaze helps people discover and access venues, invitations, passes, and event-linked services more directly, with less back-and-forth and fewer hidden layers.

This creates a product story around:

- direct access
- clearer pricing
- faster decisions
- better trust
- lower coordination cost

## Why This Matters

For guests and organizers:

- they can compare venues and event services without relying on a broker
- they can move from discovery to invitation to pass in one flow
- they can understand what they are getting more clearly
- they may pay less because fewer manual commissions are involved

For venues:

- they can receive more direct demand
- they can present spaces, services, pricing, and rules more clearly
- they can reduce dependence on informal sales channels
- they can keep a stronger relationship with the customer

For Kaze:

- this is a strong and practical market wedge
- it turns Kaze into a distribution and transaction layer, not just a utility app
- it supports long-term revenue through software fees, commissions, featured placement, and owned service businesses

## Important Product Framing

Kaze should not say:

- "We remove all middlemen."
- "We guarantee the cheapest price."

Kaze should say:

- Kaze makes venue access more direct.
- Kaze reduces unnecessary coordination layers.
- Kaze helps customers and venues connect more transparently.
- Kaze can reduce cost and confusion compared to broker-heavy flows.

This wording is stronger, safer, and more credible.

## Good App Store Angles

These are useful directions for Play Store or App Store messaging:

- Book and access venues more directly
- Invitations, passes, and venue details in one place
- Clearer event and venue coordination
- Less back-and-forth, fewer hidden layers
- Discover event spaces and services without the usual chaos

## Messaging You Can Reuse Later

### Short Version

Kaze helps people reach venues and event services more directly, with clearer pricing, smoother coordination, and less reliance on informal middlemen.

### Website Version

Kaze brings venue discovery, invitations, access passes, and event-linked services into one experience. Instead of relying on fragmented manual coordination or commission-based middlemen, customers and venues can connect more directly, move faster, and work with better transparency.

### Play Store Style Version

Find venues, invitations, passes, and event services in one place. Kaze helps guests, organizers, and venues connect more directly, with clearer details and less coordination friction.

## Should This Be In Onboarding?

Yes, but carefully.

I would not make it the first onboarding message.
The first onboarding should explain what the app does in a simple visual way.

This positioning is better used in:

- the app store description
- the website
- a later onboarding slide
- an About / Why Kaze page
- investor and partnership materials

Best onboarding version:

- "Reach venues more directly"
- "Less back-and-forth"
- "Clearer pricing and access"

That works better than a long explanation about brokers or commissions.

## Future Expansion This Supports

This market position can expand naturally into:

- direct venue booking
- transparent event service pricing
- organizer dashboards
- verified vendor marketplaces
- deposits and payments inside Kaze
- owned service businesses later

Examples:

- Kaze Studio
- Kaze Transport
- Kaze Print and Branding
- Kaze Event Styling

## Long-Term Profit Logic

Over time, Kaze can make money from:

- venue listing and lead fees
- booking commissions
- event software subscriptions
- featured service placement
- payment fees
- owned service operations in the highest-demand categories

## Final Position

Kaze is not just an event app.
It can become a more direct market infrastructure layer for venues, organizers, guests, and service providers.

That is a strong story for Rwanda and for expansion later.
