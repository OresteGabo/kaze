# Rwanda Venture Ideas

## Purpose

This file captures business ideas that fit Rwanda and East Africa, but should not all be forced into Kaze itself.

The goal is to help Kaze stay focused while keeping a long-term founder roadmap for future companies, platforms, and vertical integrations.

## How To Use This File

When an idea appears attractive, ask:
- should this strengthen Kaze directly now?
- should this stay a partner service for now?
- should this become a separate company later?

## 1. Kaze Studio

### What it is

A photo, video, and live-streaming business focused on events.

### Why it fits Rwanda

- events rely heavily on media
- weddings and conferences both spend on visuals
- quality and reliability matter a lot
- media services can command premium pricing

### How it connects to Kaze

At first:
- list third-party photographers and videographers inside Kaze
- take commissions

Later:
- operate Kaze-owned teams for premium events
- offer preferred vendor packages
- bundle media with passes, invitations, or event packages

### Growth path

Year 1:
- marketplace and partner referrals

Year 2:
- curated premium providers

Year 3+:
- own branded media crews and editing packages

### Profit logic

- commission revenue
- premium package margins
- upsells for drone, reels, live stream, same-day edits

## 2. Kaze Transport

### What it is

A transport coordination business for guests, VIPs, speakers, and wedding parties.

### Why it fits Rwanda

- events often need reliable movement between airport, hotel, venue, and after-party locations
- transport is stressful and fragmented
- trust and punctuality matter

### How it connects to Kaze

At first:
- transport booking as an event add-on

Later:
- dedicated Kaze shuttle or premium fleet
- airport-to-event packages
- speaker and VIP transport coordination

### Growth path

Year 1:
- partner drivers and small fleets

Year 2:
- preferred transport network with standards

Year 3+:
- direct Kaze-managed transport operations

### Profit logic

- booking commissions
- premium service margins
- subscription contracts with organizers or venues

## 5. Kaze Venue OS

### What it is

A B2B software layer for venues, not a guest app.

### Why it fits Rwanda

- many venues still operate with fragmented tools
- there is room for local software that understands local payments and event workflows

### How it connects to Kaze

This is the backend or business platform direction behind Kaze, but it may eventually deserve its own product identity.

It can power:
- venue setup
- event layouts
- access control
- service coordination
- organizer tools

### Growth path

Year 1:
- internal tooling for Kaze

Year 2:
- venue-facing dashboard

Year 3+:
- standalone SaaS for venues and event operators

### Profit logic

- SaaS subscriptions
- per-event fees
- enterprise setup and support fees

Year 2:

Year 3+:

## Which Ideas Belong Inside Kaze Now

Best to keep inside Kaze now:
- ticketing and passes
- invitations and RSVP
- event-linked services marketplace
- venue guidance
- local event payments

## Which Ideas Should Start As Partners

Best to keep as partner categories first:
- photography and video
- transport
- decor and styling
- printing and branding

## Which Ideas Could Become Separate Businesses Later

Strong future standalone directions:
- Kaze Studio
- Kaze Transport
- Kaze Event Styling
- Kaze Print And Branding
- Kaze Venue OS
- Kaze Wedding Platform

## Founder Strategy Recommendation

The smartest sequence is:

1. Make Kaze itself very focused and useful.
2. Use Kaze to generate demand for event services.
3. Learn which service categories have the strongest margins and most repeat demand.
4. Own the best categories later through separate branded businesses or operating units.

## Final Rule

Kaze should be the demand engine and coordination layer first.

You do not need to own every service on day one.
You need to own the customer relationship, the event flow, and the best place to capture demand.
